Before Getting Started

Before attempting to use Pushing Taboo or any darknet market, there’s a few subjects which require something of an understanding of in order to have a successful experience.

  • Tor. You must know how to use a Tor browser, which is a specially-configured version of Mozilla Firefox that allows users to visit .onion websites (the “dark web”).
  • Bitcoin. You must know how to transact BTC, which is the only form of payment accepted at Pushing Taboo. This includes sending and receiving it, as well as how to assign an appropriate transaction fee.
  • PGP. You must know how to 1) create your own PGP key pair, 2) find your public key, 3) decrypt messages sent to you with your private key, and 4) encrypt messages sent to others with their public key.
  • OpSec. You must have at least a basic understanding of what OpSec (operation security) entails. This includes things like knowing how to maintain your anonymity online and how to anonymize your Bitcoin before making a transaction.*

*at the very least, don’t send BTC straight from an exchange to a Pushing Taboo address, as that behavior is not just bad OpSec but also considered ‘taboo’, pun intended.
